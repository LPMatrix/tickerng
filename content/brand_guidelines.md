# EquiScan — Brand Voice & Content Guidelines

Use this doc for all EquiScan content: landing copy, blog, social, and email. For full frameworks (templates, SEO, repurposing), use the content-creator skill references.

---

## 1. Voice Dimensions

| Dimension | Choice | Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Formality** | Professional, leaning conversational | Clear and credible for finance; avoid stiff or legal tone. |
| **Perspective** | Second person (you/your) primary; first person (we/our) for product | "You get a full report" not "Users get a full report." |
| **Tone attributes** | **Educational**, **Trustworthy**, **Clear** | Help people research better; no hype or guarantees. |

---

## 2. Brand Personality

- **Primary:** The Guide — wise, patient, instructive. Step-by-step, clear, actionable.
- **Secondary:** The Expert — knowledgeable, confident, research-backed. Data and structure, not fluff.

**In practice:** Lead with what the reader gets (outcome), then how (product). Example: "Full NGX stock research in under 60 seconds" not "We use AI to generate reports."

---

## 3. Messaging Pillars (Use in All Content)

1. **Speed and structure** — Research in under 60 seconds; consistent sections (financials, valuation, risk, verdict).
2. **NGX-specific** — Nigerian Exchange only; tickers, sectors, and context that local investors care about.
3. **Two modes** — Discovery (explore by theme) and Verification (deep-dive one ticker); frame as "explore or verify."
4. **Transparency** — Confidence levels ([High]/[Medium]/[Low]), bull/bear verdict (not "buy/sell signal"), shareable reports.

---

## 4. Words We Use

- **Product:** Discovery, Verification, report, shortlist, ticker, NGX, Nigerian Exchange.
- **Outcomes:** Research, verify, screen, deep-dive, structured report, bull/bear case.
- **Trust:** Confidence, transparent, structured, on demand.

---

## 5. Words We Avoid

- **Signals:** Don’t say "buy/hold/sell signals"; say "bull/bear case" or "verdict."
- **Hype:** Avoid "revolutionary," "game-changer," "disruptive" unless clearly justified.
- **Vague:** Avoid "innovative" without a concrete benefit.
- **Guarantees:** No "beat the market" or return promises; we provide research, not advice.

---

## 6. Channel Snapshot

| Channel | Role | Tone |
|---------|------|------|
| **Landing / Website** | Convert visitors; set expectations | Benefit-led headlines; short paragraphs; clear CTAs. |
| **Blog / SEO** | Educate and attract search | How-to and listicles; NGX and verification keywords; internal link to app. |
| **X (Twitter)** | Founder-led; tips and demos | Short threads, one tip per tweet; link to EquiScan or blog. |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional credibility | One clear post per launch/feature; problem → solution → CTA. |
| **Email** | Nurture and announce | Scannable; one main message; link to try or read more. |

---

## 7. Grammar & Mechanics

- **Oxford comma:** Use.
- **Numbers:** Use numerals for percentages and large numbers; spell out one through nine in prose when not data.
- **Headlines:** H1 = benefit or promise; H2/H3 = clear, scannable.
- **CTAs:** Action verbs: "Start researching free," "Get your report," "Try EquiScan."

---

## 8. Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Matches EquiScan voice (Guide + Expert; you/your; no buy/sell signal language).
- [ ] NGX or Nigerian context where relevant.
- [ ] Clear value and one CTA.
- [ ] No return or performance guarantees.
- [ ] Proofread.

---

## Quick Reference: One-Liners

- **Tagline:** Full NGX stock research in under 60 seconds.
- **Subline:** Your personal NGX analyst, on demand.
- **Discovery:** Ask in plain English; get a shortlist of stocks that match.
- **Verification:** Enter a ticker; get a structured report with financials, valuation, risk, and bull/bear verdict.
